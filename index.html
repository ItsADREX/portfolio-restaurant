Our Story

Born from long evenings
and old recipes.

Solène began as a private supper club — twelve seats, one chef, and a record player. Today, the table is bigger, but the soul is the same: smoke, citrus, slow conversation, and dishes that taste like somewhere your grandmother once cooked.

Every plate honours the West African pantry — egusi, suya spice, locust bean, bitter leaf — paired with technique drawn from Lyon, Lisbon and Dakar. The result is unmistakably Lagos, dressed for the evening.

— Chef Adaeze Iroka
